Why AI Education Matters Now

69%

Kids Use AI For School

69% of high school students reported using ChatGPT to help with school assignments and homework in May 2025.

Source: College Board

50%

Kids Feel Uncertain About AI

Students are on the fence: 50% neither agree nor disagree that the benefits of using AI are greater than the risks, one-quarter agree, and one-quarter disagree. Among parents of high school students, nearly 6 in 10 agree it's better for students to use GenAI for schoolwork than not to use it.

Source: College Board

85%

AI Learning is Valuable

More than 85% of school administrators view students' learning to use AI tools as part of their high school education as very or somewhat valuable.

Source: College Board

25%

Parents Still Have used Generative AI

Parents and teens differed in their reported use of and response to generative AI. Just one in four parents reported using generative AI tools like ChatGPT. This creates an education gap.

Source: CSBA Blog

262%

Job postings requiring AI skills

Skills in artificial intelligence are appearing in more job descriptions than ever. Job postings requiring AI skills jumped 73% from 2023 to 2024 and surged another 109% from 2024 to 2025. The number jumped 13% from June to July 2025 alone.

Source: edX

78%

Organizations Use AI

A recent McKinsey study found that 78% of organizations use AI in at least one business function, up from just 50% in 2022, making these skills essential for staying competitive.

Source: edX

What is the Young and Hungry Podcast?

The Young and Hungry podcast was created for students that turns classroom topics into captivating conversations. We explore how technology and learning shape the future. It gives high school students a space to think out loud, ask meaningful questions, and learn from people with different perspectives. We believe in podcasts as a vessel for growth, not a shortcut.
